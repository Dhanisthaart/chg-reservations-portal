Call Handling Excellence

Aspire Test Call

ASPIRE TEST CALL

  • The telephone call was answered within 3 rings or 10 seconds with an appropriate greetings i.e. "Good Morning, thank you for calling Property Name, this is Agent's Name from Reservations. How may I assist you?"
  • Reservationist obtains the caller's name and clarify spelling where required. i.e. "How may I address you?". Caller's name is used naturally as a sign of recognition, throughout the duration of the call - 3 times minimum. Agent should only reconfirm the spelling upon booking confirmation
  • Telephone conversation is calm and clear. i.e. Free of background noise and or disturbance (i.e. makes the conversation difficult to hear or causes distractions.) Agents must use a headset for all guest calls
  • The guest is always asked permission before being placed on hold. i.e "Guest Name, may I place the call on hold for a moment while I confirm our availability during your requested dates?"
  • No telephone hold longer than 30 seconds without offering call-back.
  • Agent must clarify if the guest had stayed before i.e. "[Guest Name], would this stay be your first with Property Name?"
    If Yes:
    • Extend warm welcome, i.e. "We are thrilled to have you with us for the first time. We will do everything we can to ensure your stay is exceptional."
    If No:
    • Extend a warm welcome back, i.e."It is wonderful to welcome you back, [Guest Name]! We are delighted you have chosen to stay with us again. As a returning guest, please be assured we are committed to making this visit even more memorable than the last."
  • If the requested dates were unavailable, alternative dates or room option is offered.
  • Uncover the guest's needs and purpose of visit by asking at least one (1) open-ended question. i.e. "Will you be celebrating a special occasion, during your time with us in Property Name?"
  • Transitional phrase that positively reinforces that you can help the guest. Examples: "I would be delighted to assist you" or "It would be my pleasure to see what we have available."
  • Agent provides a hotel introduction by describing property facilities and procedures with clarity, make necessary recommendations based on needs/expectations, information provided should be relevant to the guest's needs/purpose of stay. (For returning guests, agents should provide information that is most updated and relevant to the guest's needs and purpose of stay.)
  • Clarify if the guest had any personal preferences (e.g. bed preference, smoking preference etc.)
  • Agent presents 2 choices of room types when available and/or appropriate using benefit statements and powerful words based on the need, wants and expectations before presenting the rates.
  • Agent can readily describe the various physical elements in the guest room and benefits to guests, by room type
  • Agent inquires if guest is a DISCOVERY Member prior to providing a rate quote
    If Yes:
    • Show recognition and appreciation, for example, "Thank you for your loyalty as a GHA DISCOVERY member"
    • Offer the member rate, for example, "I am pleased to offer you the exclusive members only rate for this stay"
    • Share ONE fact about member benefits, such as DISCOVERY Dollars, for example, "You will be able to earn and redeem your DISCOVERY Dollars during your stay"
    If No:
    • Offer membership enrollment.
    • Share member benefits, such as the member rate and DISCOVERY Dollars.
  • When appropriate presented a rate plan and/or package that would meet the needs, wants and expectations of the caller.
  • Agent clearly state the room rate and what it included/excluded (i.e. breakfast and living room access)
  • Agent confidently asked for sale. i.e. "[Guest Name] would you like me to proceed with the reservation?"
    If the guest responds with a "Yes" to the reservation query:
    • "Excellent [Guest Name], I will proceed with the reservation."
    If the guest responds with a "No":
    • Enquire if guest would like to review alternate room types, date or rate plans
    • If guest still would not like to proceed - "Certainly, [Guest Name], I am here to assist you as needed. If you have any questions or if there is anything you would like to know please feel free to ask."
  • Agent obtained caller's contact details (telephone number and email address)
  • Agent emphasises on the value, clearly explains deposit, flexible cancellation policies, penalty charge and etc.
  • Agent advises on payment options and obtains preference for payment mode to guarantee reservation (e.g. credit card etc.)
  • Ascertain the expected time of arrival, ask for flight details and advise the check in time (in the case where the guest was an early arrival).
  • Agent can effectively provide directions and/or transportation options and their associated costs. Agent should offer to assist with transportation reservations when relevant.
  • Agent offers hotel services, such as hotel/resort programme, dinner or spa reservations.
  • A reservation confirmation number is offered.
  • Agent advises that a pre-arrival email will be sent prior to guest arrival.
  • Details of the booking are repeated for confirmation. Reservation recap to include dates and day of the stay, room types, rates, requests, method of payment, cancellation and deposit requirement
  • Agent specifically thanks the guest for the booking and closes the interaction with a polite and appropriate remark. i.e. "Thank you [Guest's name], Is there anything else I may assist you with today?" followed by "It was a pleasure to assist you with your reservation. Once again, my name is Agent's Name from the reservation department. We look forward to welcome you on Arrival Date. Good bye".
  • Confirmation email is accurate
  • Confirmation is offered and received within 2 hours of the call.
  • The guest is fully prepared for their stay after the call, and they look forward to their visit with positive anticipation
  • Colleague / Teammate is highly articulate and avoids slang and excessive use of phrase-fragments
  • Colleague / Teammate is polite and maintains a gracious tone and appropriate pace throughout the interaction
  • Colleague / Teammate exhibits a genuine sense of interest and concern for the guest
  • Colleague / Teammate leads the conversation and is thoughtful and intuitive, demonstrating anticipatory service when appropriate and helpful
  • The service is handled without excessive delays or interruptions
